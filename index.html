The Pitch

Sal Sagan is 18, just out of high school, interning at an engineering firm. He has no superpower. He has dress shoes with deployable wheels and micro-thrusters, a suit jacket with serrated collar lining, a pill he engineered himself — and five years of judo. In a world full of people with actual abilities, he keeps winning.

The World

Superpowers exist. Most people have minor ones — strength enhancement, skin hardening, regeneration. The kind that tip a street fight from unfair to impossible. Sal has none of these. He has better tools.

The Hook

Every fight reveals a gap in his system. He goes home, fixes the gap, and comes back. The story is about what happens when you apply engineering-level rigor to violence — and what that costs over time.

The Stakes

The compound formula starts working too well. The dose creeps up. By the time he's facing opponents who are actually dangerous, the chemistry might be the most dangerous thing in his corner.

The Destination

Saul Rayes — "Triple-S" — has never lost. He's been in this story since page one as a blurry figure at the edge of a second-place trophy photo. Everything Sal builds is pointing at him without knowing it.

"He checks the pulse of every person he puts down. He props them against the wall and makes sure their airway is clear. Not because anyone is watching. Just because he does."

— Chapter 3 director's note. This is who he is.
